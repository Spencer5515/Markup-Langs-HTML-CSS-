Katherine Johnson

NASA Computer and Mathematician

Katherine Johnson sitting in her desk at the
Langley Memorial Aeronautical Laboratory


Here's a timeline of Mrs. Johnsons's life:
  • 1918 - Born in White Sulphur Springs, West Virginia.
  • 1929 - Enrolls in a highschool in Institute, West Virginia at the age of 10.
  • 1933 - Graduates highschool at the age of 14 and enrolls at West Virginia State, a historically black college.
  • 1933-1937 - Takes every mathematics course offered by the college. She was mentored by many professors, including the third African-American to recieve a Ph.D. in Mathematics.
  • 1937 - Graduates at the age of 18 as summa cum laude with Bachelor's degrees in Mathematics and French
  • 1937 - She takes on a job as a teacher at a black public school in Virginia.
  • 1939 - After quitting her job as a teacher, Mrs. Johnson enrolls in a graduate mathematics program. She quits one year later after deciding to focus on her pregnancy and family. She was the only woman and one of three African-American students selected to integrate into the college at the time.
  • 1940-1952 - Johnson decided on a career as a research mathemaatician, although jobs in the field were few and far between.
  • 1952-1953 - She began considering a job at the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (NASA's predacessor) and later accepted an offer to work as a computer in June of 1953.
  • 1953-1958 - Johnson works as a computer under the West Area Computing Group which was managed by Dorothy Vaughn, a soon-to-be longtime friend of Johnson. During her time as a computer, she also worked in Langely's Fligth Research Division with a staff of white male engineers and, as a result, had to stand her ground and work hard to make a name for herself both as a woman and an African-American.
  • 1957 - Johnsons provides some of the mathematics for a document known as Notes on Space Technology which combined a series of lectures by engineers in various divisions at NACA.
  • 1960 - Johnson and Ted Skopinski coauthor a report that discussed the equations needed for orbital spacefluight with a unique landing position, this report was called Determination of Azimuth Angle at Burnout for Placing a Satellite Over a Selected Earth Position.
  • 1961 - She performs the trajectory analysis of Alan Shepards mission Freedom 7 spaceflight.
  • 1962 - As shown in the famous movie, Hidden Figures, Johnson performed emergency calculations for John Glenn's mission when the computer was not trustworthy enough, by Glenn's standards.
  • 1962-1986 - Johnson goes on to author 26 research reports and to perform calculations for the Apollo 11 moon landing, the Landsat program,and the first space shuttle.
  • 1986 - She retires from NASA after 33 years
  • 2015 - Johnson is awarded the Presidental Medal of Freedom by then President Barack Obama.
  • 2020 - She passes away at the age of 101 years old on February 24, 2020.
